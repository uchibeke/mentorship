Step 1: Submit a Request

If you're faced with a tough bug, or just want some guidance, help is only a text away! Text (657) 242-2537 (6572-HACKER) with your table number (should be on a paper on your table) and any tags or technologies that describe your problem. It can be specific ("angular") or broad ("design"). Example: 456 css angularjs.

If you need to cancel your request for any reason, just text cancel!

Step 2: Wait for Your Mentor

It might take a couple minutes, but we'll match you with a mentor who has experience with your technology. We'll even give preference to mentors you've worked with before.

As long as you stay at your seat, a mentor will come directly to you. Look for the hint about their appearance.

Step 3: Close Your Request

Once your problem is solved, let us know you're finished by replying with anything. Literally anything. Write us a poem! (By the way, your mentor can also close it for you.)

Step 1: Send Your Passphrase

Text (415) 763-6867 (4157-MENTOR) with the passphrase you recieved over email. Don't worry about capitalization! After this, you're officially in our system.

And if you're having any issues at all, simply call that number: it connects directly to the Director of Mentorship's personal cell.

Step 2: Set Your Settings

Click the link we sent you. Here, you can change your name and tell hackers about what you look like. Try to be as descriptive as possible!

This is also where you set up your tags. We've pre-filled a nice list, but you can also enter a custom tag if you want! You'll get tickets that match any of your tags.

Finally, if you want to take a break, you can turn off the texts right here. (You can also just text off at any time.)

Remember to hit Save!

Step 3: Recieve a Ticket

We'll do all the work of finding you tickets -- we'll even prioritize hackers you've worked with before. Once there's a match, you'll get a fancy-shmancy picture message that includes the tags, seat location, and a spiffy map of the hacker's location. The floor plans are a tiny bit inaccurate, but it shouldn't be too hard to find your hacker.

Step 4: Claim a Ticket

If you're not interested in the ticket, just leave it be -- we'll pass it along to another mentor within a minute. But if you're up for it, respond with literally anything (write us a poem!) and the ticket is yours. Once you accept the ticket, the hacker will get a text letting them know you're on the way.

Step 5: Close a Ticket

Once the problem is solved, let us know you're finished by replying with anything. (The hacker can also close it for you.)
